About Me

I'm Tolu a Senior FX Analyst at a Big Five Canadian Bank with 7+ years of institutional financial operations experience. I specialise in high-accuracy, high-volume environments: multi-currency FX reconciliation, Visa dispute management, fraud analysis, and the process design that makes complex operations scale without breaking.

My career started in Lagos supervising construction projects, reconciling sales accounts for Nigerian Breweries, processing government payment vouchers, and selling Ford vehicles in a showroom that taught me the most important lesson of my professional life: diagnose the real problem before you open a laptop.

That discipline followed me to Toronto and into a Big Five Canadian Bank through chargebacks, Visa pre-arbitration, fraud analysis, and now FX settlements across 15+ currencies. Four progressive roles inside the same institution Chargeback Analyst, Business Systems Analyst, Fraud Analyst, Senior FX Analyst each building directly on the one before. I don't just operate financial systems I redesign the ones that leak.

I hold a Certified Scrum Master (CSM) certification and post-graduate certificates in International Business Management (Georgian College) and Project Management (Humber College). The PM path started at Georgian, where I did a co-op placement, delivered SOPs, and documented system walkthroughs that experience pointed me toward Humber's IT Project Management program, which led directly to the Scrum certification and how I now run every project.

Outside work, I care deeply about teaching people and improving lives through technology. I follow AI and IoT closely not as industry trends but as tools that genuinely change what's possible for individuals. I play video games not to escape but because good games are the best systems design you'll encounter outside an engineering department.
